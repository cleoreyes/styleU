Problem/Data Domain

Overview

  1. The rise of mass consumerism and fashion experimentation leads to overproduction and waste in the fashion industry, which harms the environment. Brands make more clothes than needed to meet demand, using up resources and causing pollution. This results in a lot of discarded clothing, especially with fast fashion, adding to global waste. It also often involves exploiting workers, particularly in developing countries. To tackle these issues, consumers can choose more sustainable habits, and the industry needs to adopt transparent and responsible practices to reduce the environmental and social impact of mass consumerism in fashion.
  2. In the space of fashion and style, information is scattered on a plethora of different resources which makes it difficult to assess what is best for each individual and keep track. Additionally, making an available version for all to access would be beneficial as these quizzes and consultations are usually a service that is only provided in specific countries. Importantly, addressing the topic of mass consumerism and waste in the fashion industry is something that is pushed to the side, and through this project we would be able to put it in the forefront of individuals and educate them,
  3. One existing attempt to address fashion style is a website called “Fashmate” which allows users to create their own outfits and styles and share them with the community. While this solution may be useful, it doesn’t address mass consumerism and waste which is a prevalent issue in today’s society.
